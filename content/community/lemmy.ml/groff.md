Groff (GNU troff) is a typesetting system that reads plain text mixed with formatting commands and produces formatted output. Output may be PostScript or PDF, html, or ASCII/UTF-8 for display at the terminal. Present on most Unix systems owing to its long association with Unix manuals (manpages), groff is capable of producing typographically sophisticated documents while consuming only minimal system resources.<br>-- from GNU<br><br>(link: https://www.gnu.org/software/groff/)<br><br>Feel free to ask questions about the roff family and its many macros! Interested in learning more? Type `man groff` or `info groff` into your terminal to read the documentation.
