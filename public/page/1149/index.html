A community for fans of the Shingeki no Kyojin (Attack on Titan) manga and anime, written by Hajime Isayama and produced by Studio WiT (seasons 1-3) and MAPPA (season 4). **Rules:** - No unmarked spoilers! You must mark them with Lemmy's built-in spoiler tag and indicate in *both the post title and the spoiler title* what they pertain to (Manga chapters which haven't been adapted yet, Anime episodes, etc.), as such: ::: spoiler [Manga Spoilers] Made you look! ;) ::: - Additionally, if the post contains an image, you must either include it in the body behind a spoiler tag or mark the post as NSFW, otherwise it will be shown by default to users. Unmarked spoilers will be removed. - No spoilers in the title itself are allowed. - Be civil, you must adhere to Lemmy's Code of Conduct at all times. - If you post fan artwork which you haven't made, you must *always* credit the original author, either by linking to the original author's page directly or by stating it in a comment. - Sharing or endorsing unofficial/pirated distributions of the manga/anime is not allowed. We should support the official release. Note that sharing chapter leaks before the chapter is released internationally *is* allowed for the sake of discussion, but this can only be done in the comments of discussion threads and should be removed after the chapter officially releases.

Attitudinal Healing
Thursday, June 15, 2023

PRINCIPLES OF ATTITUDINAL HEALING The Principles are fundamental to Attitudinal Healing. The first Principle is the key. It unites and flows through all the others. As the Principles are practiced, it is evident that they overlap and each includes aspects of all the other Principles. The Principles are not dogma. Spiritual in nature, they are nonsectarian and are offered only as tools. The essence of our being is love. Health is inner peace. Healing is letting go of fear. Giving and receiving are the same. We can let go of the past and of the future. Now is the only time there is and each instant is for giving. We can learn to love ourselves and others by forgiving rather than judging. We can become love finders rather than fault finders. We can choose and direct ourselves to be peaceful inside regardless of what is happening outside. We are students and teachers to each other. We can focus on the whole of life rather than the fragments. Since love is eternal, death need not be viewed as fearful. We can always perceive ourselves and others as either extending love or giving a call of help for love.
