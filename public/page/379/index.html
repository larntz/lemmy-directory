Rabbits and Hats, what could be better?
Friday, June 16, 2023

It's like a specialized /r/Rabbits . Bunnies with hats only. Hats can be unconventional -- even other bunnies. Rules: 1. All images should include a bunny with a hat. We mean hat in the loosest sense of the object. Acceptable hats include vegetables, wigs, and toys, among others. 2. No vote solicitation. e.g. /c/aww didn't like my bunny, Can I get some love.., etc. Your rabbit will gain karma based on their merit by being a bunny. 3. Post sources of non-original content. Clearly indicate in the title if a post is a cross-post. Leave a comment with credit or best guess at a source if a post is not personal original content. Posts that do not follow these rules may be removed without warning. 4. No obvious or regular defensive willful negligence of pet rabbits. Any obvious posts of neglectful pet rabbit welfare (including but not limited to small cages, wire flooring, untreated medical issues, etc.) will be removed. If there is regular evidence of poor pet rabbit care after receiving repeated feedback from the community, a user may have their posts removed and be banned based on moderator discretion. 5. No image posts about bathing rabbits or handling rabbits on their backs. Bathing and trancing can cause unnecessary stress to rabbits and are not recommended unless medically necessary due to the dangers of the hold. Ambiguous photos of rabbits placed on their backs may also be removed even if the rabbit is not fully tranced. Originally [r/BunniesWithHats](https://old.reddit.com/r/bunnieswithhats/)

RCT
Friday, June 16, 2023

Migration of the Reddit community /r/RCT. Focused on the classic roller coaster sim games, RCT1, RCT2, RCT3, RCTC and the open-source OpenRCT2. # **Rules** Please DO post: - Your own content. - Your own streams and Let's Plays. - Feedback and advice. - News about RCT games or OpenRCT. Please do NOT post: - Harassment of other users. Abide by the [code of conduct](https://join-lemmy.org/docs/en/code_of_conduct.html). - NSFW content. - Any non-RCT-related content. This includes content titled or captioned to be RCT-related as well as anything related to real life coasters or theme parks. - Advice animals or memes. Take those to [/c/rctcirclejerk](https://lemmy.world/c/rctcirclejerk) instead. - Murdercoasters. - Rides named for guest reactions. - Rides with outrageous ratings and nothing else. - Ride statistics without context. - TIL and PSA posts. If you are asking for help, please try to be as descriptive as possible! I haven't figured out an equivalent to the /r/RCT wiki yet, but at some point the plan is to work on porting that too.
